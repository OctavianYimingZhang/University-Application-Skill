# Application Readiness

Use this workflow for application materials, portal steps, tests, references, fees, funding, deadlines, submission review, and administrative student-visa preparation.

## Readiness workflow

1. Identify the programme, application cycle, intended submission stage, and the applicant's current material inventory.
2. Retrieve current official requirements for the items being checked.
3. Compare each requirement with applicant-owned evidence.
4. Separate ready items, blockers, date-sensitive actions, optional improvements, and unknowns.
5. Check the relevant fields: programme choice, portal instructions, qualifications, transcripts, language tests, references, CV, statement or proposal, portfolio, fees, deposits, scholarships, deadlines and timezones, interviews, and mandatory pre-application steps.
6. Return the requested explanation, compact status table, blocker summary, or action list. State submission status only when the evidence confirms it.

An official requirement page establishes what is needed. A current applicant-owned document establishes that the applicant has it. Use both sources when marking an item ready.

## Visa administration

For student-visa preparation, collect destination jurisdiction, citizenship, application location, programme or sponsor, intake, and funding basis only when they affect the answer.

Use current official government or immigration-authority guidance for legal and procedural rules. Use university pages for institution-specific sponsorship documents and steps. Record jurisdiction and access date, and separate confirmed rules, applicant evidence gaps, timing risks, and questions that require the authority or a qualified immigration professional.

Provide administrative readiness information. Direct unusual immigration history, refusals, dependants, criminal matters, status changes, or legal ambiguity to the official authority or a qualified professional.

## Final check

Verify current-cycle sources, deadline timezones, required file formats, prompt limits, reference procedures, and every blocker named in the output. Keep missing or conflicting evidence visible.
