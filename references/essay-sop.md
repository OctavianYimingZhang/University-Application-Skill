# Admissions Writing

Use this workflow for SOPs, personal statements, supplemental essays, motivation letters, programme-fit paragraphs, planning, drafting, and revision.

## Brief and evidence

Read the prompt, programme, word or character limit, applicant material, existing draft, and requested output. Ask only for a missing item that changes the document.

Build a compact evidence inventory from confirmed material:

- academic background, modules, projects, papers, labs, and methods;
- employment, internships, volunteering, leadership, teaching, or service;
- skills with concrete proof;
- setbacks or changes of direction that are relevant to the prompt;
- research questions, career goals, and why the programme is the next step;
- programme facts verified from current official sources.

Use uploaded assignments, slides, readings, notes, and writing samples as knowledge, interest, or voice evidence according to `evidence-contract.md`.

## Workflow

1. Identify the prompt's required response and the applicant's strongest supported evidence.
2. Choose a central narrative that connects background, evidence, goals, and the programme.
3. Map each paragraph to a purpose and supporting evidence. Show narrative options first only when the choice would materially change the document.
4. Verify programme-specific claims, including relevant supervisors, research, representative publications, modules, facilities, or structure.
5. Draft or revise in the applicant's voice. Preserve confirmed facts and intellectual ownership while improving specificity, logic, organisation, and discipline fit.
6. For multiple applications, preserve confirmed shared evidence while adapting the prompt response and programme fit for each target.
7. Review unsupported claims, generic programme fit, weak causal links, repetition, tone, length, and prompt coverage.

When revision instructions conflict, preserve each instruction and ask the applicant to choose before editing the affected passage. Merge exact duplicates and implement confirmed changes at their intended location.

## Quality

Make interests concrete through a specific question, concept, method, or experience. Keep programme customisation substantive enough that changing only the institution name would not preserve the paragraph.

The final document should contain supported applicant claims, current programme facts, a clear response to the prompt, and no invented achievements or experiences.
