# Visa Readiness

Use this focused Skill to identify student-visa preparation gaps without giving legal advice or predicting an immigration outcome.

## Workflow

1. Confirm destination jurisdiction, citizenship, intended application location, programme or sponsor, application cycle, intake, and funding basis when they affect the route.
2. Read the package [research rules](../../references/research.md), [evidence contract](../../references/evidence-contract.md), and [quality checks](../../references/quality-checks.md).
3. Collect the current official government or immigration-authority page for every legal or procedural rule. Use an official university page only for institution-specific sponsorship or document steps.
4. Record each claim with source URL, title, publisher, application cycle, access date, source availability, fact verification, completeness, and staleness.
5. Separate confirmed rules, applicant evidence gaps, timing risks, and questions that require the official authority or a qualified immigration professional.
6. Return a readiness checklist, source log, and concise statement that the output is administrative readiness information rather than legal advice. Never imply that a visa will be issued.

## Boundary

- Provide administrative readiness information, not legal advice, legal interpretation, or representation.
- State the jurisdiction and access date for time-sensitive guidance.
- Prefer the official government or immigration-authority source over university summaries when they differ.
- Mark conflicts and unavailable or stale sources explicitly; do not resolve them from memory.
- Refer unusual immigration history, refusals, dependants, criminal matters, status changes, or legal ambiguity to the official authority or a qualified immigration professional.

Default to English. Use another output language only when the user explicitly requests it.
